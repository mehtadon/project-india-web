India has over 250,000 ward-level divisions across its cities and villages. These are the smallest units of elected governance — and yet, most citizens cannot name their ward councillor.

This disconnect is the core problem. National elections get all the attention, but the pothole outside your house, the garbage collection schedule, the water supply — these are all ward-level decisions.

## The Opportunity

If even 50 engaged citizens per ward started attending meetings, asking questions, and tracking budgets, the ripple effect would be enormous.

Ward-level democracy is not glamorous. It's not on TV. But it is where governance actually touches your life.

## What We Can Do

- Attend ward committee meetings
- Track how ward budgets are allocated and spent
- Build a small group of engaged neighbours
- Document what works and share it publicly

The goal isn't perfection. It's participation.
