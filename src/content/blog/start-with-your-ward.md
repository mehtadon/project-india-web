You don't need permission to participate in democracy. Here's how to start.

## Step 1: Find Your Ward

Every city in India is divided into wards. Find out which ward you live in. Your municipal corporation's website usually has this information.

## Step 2: Know Your Councillor

Look up who represents your ward. What promises did they make? What committees do they sit on?

## Step 3: Attend a Meeting

Ward committee meetings are public. You have every right to attend. Bring a notebook. Listen first.

## Step 4: Talk to Your Neighbours

Governance improves when neighbours talk to each other. Start a WhatsApp group. Share what you learn from meetings.

## Step 5: Document Everything

Take notes. Share them online. Transparency is the first step toward accountability.

## Remember

You don't need to start a movement. You just need to start paying attention. One ward at a time.
